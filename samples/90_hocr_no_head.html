Compressed Air

After cleaning the Magna Punch Binding Machine in the Print and Copy area with a can of pressurised air, the machine was turned back on and a fireball appeared.

The fireball quickly dissipated and was only alight for a few seconds.

This incident highlighted the fact that when compressed air is sprayed into any electric machine, it has the potential to leave a residue of gas within the machine that can ignite when the machine is turned back on.

As a result, compressed air must not be sprayed into any electrical machine and should only be used for cleaning computer keyboards as specified on the product packaging.

RECOMMENDATIONS FOR IMPLEMENTATION

Compressed Air (any brand) must not be used to clean any print and copy equipment or other electrical machines.

Communicate this Safety Alert to all print and copy team members to ensure they are aware of the risks with the use of compressed air.

Table this Safety Alert at your next Stay Safe Team Meeting to ensure all controls have been implemented and the Safety Alert actioned.

Contact the Health & Safety Team or your Area Manger if you have any questions.
